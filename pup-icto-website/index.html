About ICTO

The Digital Backbone of PUP

The Information and Communications Technology Office (ICTO) is the central technology unit of the Polytechnic University of the Philippines. It is responsible for planning, developing, implementing, and maintaining the ICT infrastructure and systems that support the academic, administrative, and research functions of the university.

ICTO ensures that PUP remains at the forefront of digital innovation, providing reliable internet connectivity, secure email services, student information systems, and a wide range of technical support services to over 70,000 students, faculty, and staff.

Vision

A digitally empowered PUP recognized as a center of excellence in ICT education and services.

Mission

To provide innovative, reliable, and accessible ICT services that support PUP's mandate.

Function

Manage all ICT systems, infrastructure, and provide technical support across all campuses.

Governance

Enforce ICT policies, data privacy, cybersecurity protocols, and digital compliance.

Our Journey

History & Milestones

The PUP Information and Communications Technology Office (ICTO) started operations in April 2000 as the PUP IT Center. It was established to spearhead the university's digital transformation.

The center was renamed to the Information and Communications Technology Center later that year and has since evolved into the comprehensive ICTO on the main campus in Sta. Mesa, Manila.

Key institutional milestones and achievements include:

  • April 2000: The center began operations under its first director, Prof. Mely R. Luya, and was subsequently renamed the ICT Center.
  • June 2000: The center was declared a Microsoft Certified Government Technology Education Center (GTEC).
  • Early 2000 designations: The broader university was recognized by CHED as a Center of Development for Excellence in IT and designated by DOST as a Virtual Center for Technology Innovation in Information Technology.
  • Academic alignment: The creation of the center coincided with the growth of PUP's academic computing units, which previously transitioned from the Electronic Data Processing (EDP) department in 1986 into the College of Computer Management and Information Technology (now the College of Computer and Information Sciences).
  • Modern expansion: The ICTO serves as the central hub for the university's IT infrastructure, student portals, and digital services (such as the Student Information System and webmail system), ensuring continuous technological support for the entire PUP community.

Sally C. Mua serves as the current Director of the Information and Communications Technology Office (ICTO) of the Polytechnic University of the Philippines, bringing extensive experience in information technology, data communications, networking, and information systems. She plays a key role in overseeing the university’s digital infrastructure and advancing ICT-driven initiatives that support academic and administrative operations.
